Site Plan


Project Purpose

The purpose of my website is to provide people with information on various Christmas-related topics, such as the history of the holiday, a short biography on Santa Claus, a short biography on Rudolph, a countdown to Christmas, a Christmas-themed guessing game, and some Christmas-themed recipes and fun ideas.


The Audience

Who is using the site?

  • Age: Children 4-12 years old with their parents
  • Education: Preschool – Middle-school
  • Income: $0-$10/week
  • Family Status: Live with parents
  • Experience level with the topic of your site and its content: High

What is the visitor doing when he/she visits the site? How will this affect their use of the site?

  • Sitting at home using a tablet, desktop, or laptop

What actions or content is the visitor wanting? What is she trying to do? What content is he trying to find? A scenario describes the visitor’s actions.

  • How many days are left until Christmas?
  • Who is Santa Claus?
  • Who is Rudolph the Red-Nosed Reindeer?
  • I need to find out what kind of cookies I want my mom to make for Christmas.
  • I want to play a fun Christmas-themed game.

Where is the visitor located? Does the visitor’s geographic location impact the content delivery?

  • I’m at home and I want to make Christmas-themed food (or, see how many days are left until Christmas, play a Christmas-themed game, etc.)

When is the site being visited? How does the time affect the content delivery?

  • It’s 11am and I need to keep my kids entertained while I make lunch.
  • It’s December and I want to do something fun to get my children excited for Christmas.

How are users accessing the site? What are the capabilities and limitations of their device?

  • A desktop machine, fast broadband connection.
  • A laptop, fast broadband connection.
  • A tablet, using wi-fi at home.
  • An iPhone6S using 4G LTE.

Web Pages

  1. Homepage – It will have a countdown that is counting down the days until December 25th, 2016 (Christmas Day). It will also have links to the other pages of the site, and a short paragraph about what you can do on the site. This page will hold my content video.
  2. History – This page will have a history of the Christmas holiday. This page will use JSON data.
  3. Biographies – This page will have biographies of Santa Claus and Rudolph the Red-Nosed Reindeer. This page will also use JSON data.
  4. Game(s) – This page will have at least one Christmas-themed game, which will be a guessing game made using JavaScript.
  5. Ideas – This page will have fun ideas for things that families can do together to celebrate Christmas, and it will have a few Christmas-themed recipes.
  6. Site Map (not a content page) – A link to this page will be shown in place of the navigation when the user has JavaScript disabled or when it is not operational.

Usability Concepts

  • I will make sure that my site’s load-time is reasonable by sizing the images appropriately so that they can load quickly and still be seen easily.
  • I will make sure that I use contrasting colors for my text/background so that it will be easy to read the text.
  • I will make sure that my font size/spacing makes the content easy to read by testing my site to see what it looks like on various screen sizes.
  • I will give my images appropriate ALT tags so that they are identifiable by people using screen-readers.
  • The title of my site will be clearly visible on the homepage and on the other pages of the site.
  • The navigation links will be clearly labeled, it will be easy to click on them, and it will be easy to tell when the mouse is hovering over them.
  • There will be a logo near the top of all of the pages that will link to the homepage.
  • The headings on the site will be clear and concise.
  • The main copy will be short and concise since this site is geared toward children.
  • Each page will have a title that will help the user to easily be able to identify the page that they have open in their browser tabs.
